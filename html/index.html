About

This web application searches the closed captioning text of TV programs.

Enclose your search terms in curly braces. This is very important. To search for china, use {china}. You can refine your search by including special operators.

  • + matches either of the two terms. For example, {china} + {beijing} looks for both china and beijing.
  • & matches two terms that occur in the same program. For example, {china} & {russia} looks for china and russia occuring together in the same program.
  • @[optional argument] matches two terms appearing close together. For example, {china} @ {russia} looks for both china and russia within 100 characters of each other. {china} @50 {russia} looks for china and russia within 50 characters of each other.
  • ! can negate & and @. {china} !& {russia} looks for china in programs where russia does not occur. {china} !@ {russia} looks for occurences of china is not within 100 characters of russia. {china} !@50 {russia} looks for occurences of china is not within 50 characters of russia.
Parentheses can be used to build searchs of aribitrary complexity. ({china} @ {russia}) & ({afghanistan} @ {iraq}) looks for programs where both china occurs near russia and afghanistan occurs near iraq.
